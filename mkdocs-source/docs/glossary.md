# Glossary of Key Terms

Definitions of the recurring physical concepts used across the 15 experiments and 7 workshops in this guide, in alphabetical order. Each entry links back to the experiment(s) where the term is most central.

## A

**Airy disk** — The central bright spot of the diffraction pattern formed when light from a point source passes through a circular aperture (such as a lens); its radius sets the ultimate resolution limit of any focusing system. See [Focusing by a Convex Lens](experiments/convex-lens-focusing.md).

**Angular divergence (θ_div)** — The full angle by which a beam spreads as it propagates; a small divergence is the defining property of a well-collimated laser beam. See [Directionality & Beam Quality](experiments/directionality-beam-quality.md).

## C

**Coherence length (L_c)** — The maximum path difference between two beams from the same source over which a stable interference pattern is still visible; L_c = c·τ_c. See [Coherence Length & Time](experiments/coherence.md).

**Coherence time (τ_c)** — The time interval over which a light wave's phase remains predictable; inversely related to the source's spectral linewidth, τ_c ≈ 1/Δν. See [Coherence Length & Time](experiments/coherence.md).

**Compton shift (Δλ)** — The increase in wavelength of a photon after scattering elastically from a free electron; depends only on the scattering angle, not on the target material or beam intensity. See [Compton Scattering](experiments/compton-scattering.md).

**Compton wavelength (λ_C)** — The fundamental length scale h/(m_ec) ≈ 2.426 pm that sets the size of the Compton shift. See [Compton Scattering](experiments/compton-scattering.md).

## D

**Dispersion** — The variation of a material's refractive index with wavelength, responsible for a prism splitting white light into a spectrum. See [Dispersion by a Prism](experiments/prism-dispersion.md).

**Dispersive power (ω)** — A dimensionless measure of how strongly a prism material separates colours, ω = (n_v − n_r)/(n_y − 1). See [Dispersion by a Prism](experiments/prism-dispersion.md).

## F

**f-number (N_f)** — The ratio of a lens's focal length to its aperture diameter, f/D; a smaller f-number gives a tighter focused spot but a shallower depth of field. See [Focusing by a Convex Lens](experiments/convex-lens-focusing.md).

**Fringe width (β)** — The spacing between consecutive bright (or dark) fringes in an interference pattern, β = λD/d in Young's double-slit geometry. See [Young's Double-Slit Interference](experiments/youngs-double-slit.md).

## H

**Hologram** — A recording of the interference pattern between an object beam and a coherent reference beam, encoding both amplitude and phase information so that a full three-dimensional image can be reconstructed. See [Making a Hologram](experiments/holography.md).

## M

**Malus's Law** — I(θ) = I₀cos²θ, describing how the transmitted intensity through a polarizer varies with the angle θ between its transmission axis and the incoming light's polarization direction. See [Polarization & Malus's Law](experiments/polarization-malus.md).

**Monochromaticity** — How closely a light source approaches emitting a single, pure wavelength; quantified by the spectral purity Δλ/λ. See [Monochromaticity & Spectral Purity](experiments/monochromaticity-purity.md).

## O

**Optical path difference (OPD)** — The difference in the optical path length travelled by two beams before they recombine; the quantity that actually determines constructive or destructive interference. See [Michelson Interferometer](experiments/michelson-interferometer.md).

## P

**Photon** — A discrete quantum of light carrying energy E = hν and momentum p = h/λ; the particle picture of light that explains the photoelectric effect, Compton scattering and single-photon interference. See [The Photoelectric Effect](experiments/photoelectric-effect.md), [Compton Scattering](experiments/compton-scattering.md), [Single-Photon Double-Slit Build-up](experiments/single-photon-double-slit.md).

**Probability density** — In the single-photon picture of interference, the quantity that predicts where an individual photon is likely to land on the screen; accumulating many photons one-by-one reproduces the classical interference pattern. See [Single-Photon Double-Slit Build-up](experiments/single-photon-double-slit.md).

## R

**Resolving power (R)** — The ability of a diffraction grating to distinguish two closely spaced wavelengths, R = λ/Δλ = nN, where n is the diffraction order and N the number of illuminated slits. See [Single-, Double- and N-Slit Diffraction](experiments/slit-diffraction.md).

## S

**Spectral purity** — The ratio Δλ/λ of a source's linewidth to its central wavelength; smaller values indicate a more nearly monochromatic source. See [Monochromaticity & Spectral Purity](experiments/monochromaticity-purity.md).

**Stopping voltage (V₀)** — The retarding voltage just sufficient to stop even the most energetic photoelectrons from reaching the collector, giving a direct measurement of their maximum kinetic energy. See [The Photoelectric Effect](experiments/photoelectric-effect.md).

## T

**Threshold frequency (ν₀)** — The minimum light frequency below which no photoelectrons are emitted from a given metal, regardless of intensity — the key observation classical wave theory could not explain. See [The Photoelectric Effect](experiments/photoelectric-effect.md).

## W

**Work function (φ)** — The minimum energy required to free an electron from a metal surface; the energy "cost" subtracted from each absorbed photon in Einstein's photoelectric equation. See [The Photoelectric Effect](experiments/photoelectric-effect.md).
