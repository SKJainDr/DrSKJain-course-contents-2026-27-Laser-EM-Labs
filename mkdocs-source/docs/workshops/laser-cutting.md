## Workshop Brief

Cut a 5 mm mild-steel plate with a CO₂ laser using oxygen assist gas. Aim for a narrow, smooth kerf with no dross (re-solidified molten metal) adhering to the bottom edge.

**Specifications:** Material: Mild steel, 5 mm · Assist gas: O₂ · Dross-free edge

## Process Theory

In laser cutting, a continuous-wave (or high repetition-rate pulsed) laser beam melts (and, with oxygen assist gas, exothermically oxidizes) a narrow kerf through the material, while a coaxial assist-gas jet blows the molten/oxidized material out of the kerf from below.

Laser power and cutting speed together set the linear energy density delivered to the material: too much power or too slow a traverse speed overheats the kerf, allowing molten material to re-solidify on the underside as dross; too little power or too fast a traverse fails to fully penetrate the plate.

The assist-gas pressure must be high enough to clear molten material from the kerf efficiently (oxygen additionally provides exothermic heating that assists cutting of ferrous metals) but not so high as to cause turbulent melt ejection that roughens the cut edge.

## Process Parameters

| Parameter | Range | Unit |
|---|---|---|
| Laser power | 500 – 3500 | W |
| Cutting speed | 300 – 3000 | mm/min |
| Assist gas pressure | 0.5 – 10 | bar |

## Procedure

1. Record the precision score at the default power, speed and gas pressure.
2. Vary laser power alone (holding speed and gas pressure fixed) and record the precision score at each value.
3. Repeat, varying cutting speed alone, then assist-gas pressure alone.
4. Determine the parameter combination that achieves a dross-free, full-penetration cut with a 90%+ precision score.
