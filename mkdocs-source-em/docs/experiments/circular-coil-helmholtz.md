## Aim

To study the magnetic field along the axis of a single circular current loop, locate the point of maximum field, and demonstrate how a Helmholtz pair produces a highly uniform field at its centre.

## Theoretical Background

The magnetic field on the axis of a circular coil of radius R and N turns carrying current I, at an axial distance x from its centre, is B(x) = μ₀NIR² / [2(R²+x²)^(3/2)]. At the centre (x = 0) this reduces to B₀ = μ₀NI/2R, the maximum value. Two identical coils placed coaxially and separated by a distance equal to their common radius R (the 'Helmholtz' condition) produce a combined field whose second derivative with respect to x vanishes at the midpoint — making the field remarkably uniform over a useful volume between the coils, which is why Helmholtz coils are the standard tool for producing a known, controllable, uniform field for calibrating magnetometers and cancelling the Earth's field in sensitive experiments.

## Governing Formula

$$ B(x) = μ₀ N I R² / [2(R² + x²)^{3/2}] $$

| Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| B(x) | axial field at distance x from the coil centre (T) |
| N | number of turns |
| I | current (A) |
| R | coil radius (m) |
| x | axial distance from the coil's centre (m) |

## Procedure

1. For a single coil, sweep x from −30 cm to +30 cm and plot B(x); verify the peak occurs at x = 0 with value μ₀NI/2R.
2. Verify B(0) ∝ N and B(0) ∝ I by varying each independently.
3. Switch to the Helmholtz configuration with separation d = R and plot the combined field along the axis; note the flat region near the midpoint.
4. Change the separation away from d = R (e.g. d = 0.5R or 2R) and observe the field profile becoming non-uniform (dip or double-hump).
5. Estimate the useful 'uniform-field volume' (region where B stays within 1% of the central value) for the ideal Helmholtz separation.
6. Compute the theoretical central field of the Helmholtz pair and compare with the simulator's numerically-integrated value.

## Precautions

- Keep R, N and I fixed while sweeping x to isolate the axial-distance dependence cleanly.
- For the Helmholtz study, change only the coil separation d between runs, not R, N or I.
- Note the field falls off increasingly steeply for |x| ≫ R; keep this in mind when defining the 'uniform region'.
- Remember the Helmholtz condition (d = R) is optimal only for the specific criterion of a vanishing second derivative at the centre — other criteria give slightly different optimal spacings.

## Maximum Permissible Error
