## Aim

To study the dependence of a parallel-plate capacitor's capacitance on plate area, plate separation, and an inserted dielectric slab, and to determine the dielectric constant of an unknown material.

## Theoretical Background

A parallel-plate capacitor of plate area A separated by distance d in vacuum has capacitance C₀ = ε₀A/d. Filling the gap with a dielectric of relative permittivity εr increases the capacitance to C = εrε₀A/d = εr·C₀, because the bound polarization charges on the dielectric surfaces partially cancel the free-charge field, lowering the potential difference for the same stored charge. If the dielectric slab of thickness t < d only partially fills the gap, the capacitor behaves as two capacitors in series: C = ε₀A / [d − t(1 − 1/εr)]. The stored energy is U = Q²/2C = ½CV², and inserting a dielectric while the plates remain connected to a battery (constant V) increases the stored charge and energy, while at constant charge Q it lowers the voltage and the energy.

## Governing Formula

$$ C = εrε₀A/d $$

| Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| C | capacitance (F) |
| εr | relative permittivity (dielectric constant) of the medium |
| A | overlap area of the plates (m²) |
| d | plate separation (m) |

## Procedure

1. With air between the plates (εr = 1), vary the separation d and plot C vs 1/d to verify the linear relationship.
2. Fix d and vary the plate area A; plot C vs A and verify a straight line through the origin.
3. Insert dielectric slabs of known εr one at a time (paper ≈ 3, mica ≈ 6, glass ≈ 5–7) fully filling the gap and record the new capacitance; compute εr = C/C₀ for each.
4. Use an 'unknown' slab and determine its dielectric constant from the capacitance ratio.
5. At fixed Q, insert the dielectric and observe the voltage drop; at fixed V (battery connected), insert the dielectric and observe the increase in stored charge.
6. Compute and compare the stored energy U before and after dielectric insertion in both the constant-Q and constant-V cases.

## Precautions

- Ensure the dielectric slab fully and uniformly fills the gap between the plates unless deliberately studying partial filling.
- Keep the plate area and dielectric fixed while varying only the separation, and vice versa.
- Avoid short-circuiting the plates (d → 0); maintain a realistic minimum separation.
- Discharge the capacitor fully before changing the dielectric or geometry to avoid stray residual charge.

## Maximum Permissible Error

$$ ΔC/C = Δεr/εr + ΔA/A + Δd/d $$

C = εrε₀A/d is a product/quotient of three measured quantities, so fractional errors add.
