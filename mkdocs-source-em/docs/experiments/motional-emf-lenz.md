## Aim

To study the motional EMF generated by a conducting rod sliding on rails in a uniform magnetic field, verify ε = BLv, and observe the retarding (Lenz's-law) force that opposes the rod's motion when the circuit is closed.

## Theoretical Background

When a conducting rod of length L slides with velocity v perpendicular to both its length and a uniform magnetic field B, the free charges in the rod experience a magnetic force qv×B that drives them along the rod, building up an EMF ε = BLv (equivalently derivable from Faraday's law applied to the changing area swept out). If the rod completes a circuit of resistance R, a current I = ε/R flows, and this current in the field experiences a retarding force F = BIL that, by Lenz's law, always opposes the rod's motion — so an external agent must do work against this force to keep the rod moving at constant velocity, and that work exactly equals the electrical energy dissipated in R, consistent with energy conservation.

## Governing Formula

$$ ε = BLv,   I = ε/R,   F_retard = BIL = B²L²v/R $$

| Symbol | Meaning |
|---|---|
| ε | motional EMF (V) |
| B | magnetic field (T) |
| L | rail separation / rod length (m) |
| v | rod velocity (m/s) |
| R | circuit resistance (Ω) |
| F_retard | retarding force on the rod (N) |

## Procedure

1. With the circuit open (infinite R), push the rod at various speeds and confirm the measured EMF matches ε = BLv with no retarding force (no current flows).
2. Close the circuit through R and repeat; verify the current I = ε/R and observe the retarding force appearing.
3. At fixed v, vary R and verify the retarding force decreases as R increases (F ∝ 1/R).
4. At fixed R, vary B and verify the retarding force grows as B² (both the EMF and current scale with B).
5. Push the rod at constant velocity against the retarding force and verify the input mechanical power equals the electrical power I²R dissipated in the resistor.
6. Reverse the direction of the rod's motion and confirm both the EMF polarity and the retarding force direction reverse consistently with Lenz's law.

## Precautions

- Ensure the rod remains perpendicular to both the rails and the field direction throughout its motion.
- Keep the rails frictionless and resistanceless in the model so only R limits the current, isolating the physics of interest.
- Vary only one parameter (B, L, v, or R) at a time when tabulating results.
- Note the retarding force acts only while the circuit is closed and current is flowing — an open circuit gives EMF but no force.
