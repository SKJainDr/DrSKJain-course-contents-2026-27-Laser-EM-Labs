# Advanced Electricity & Magnetism Learning Lab

Welcome to the User Guide for the **Advanced Electricity & Magnetism Learning Lab**, a companion virtual laboratory
covering Electrostatics, Magnetostatics, Electrodynamics, Electromagnetic Waves & their propagation in different
media, and the electric & magnetic properties of materials, at the undergraduate and postgraduate level.

!!! tip "Try it live"
    Every experiment and workshop below has a matching interactive panel in the simulator:
    **[🔬 Launch the Simulator](../../labs/advanced-electricity-magnetism-lab.html){target=_blank}**

This guide provides, for each of the **18 experiments**: the theoretical background, governing formula, apparatus
schematic, a step-by-step procedure, a blank observation table and graph grid for your own readings, a maximum
permissible error calculator, precautions, and viva-voce questions — followed by **7 hands-on industrial
workshops** that let you apply the same physics to realistic engineering scenarios, each scored against an optimal
target with a printable completion certificate above 90%.

## Topics Covered

| Module | Experiments |
|---|---|
| **Electrostatics** | Coulomb's Law · Electric Dipole Field & Potential · Gauss's Law · Parallel-Plate Capacitor & Dielectrics · Conductors & the Van de Graaff Generator |
| **Magnetostatics** | Biot–Savart Law · Circular Coil & Helmholtz Coils · Ampere's Law (Solenoid & Toroid) · Force Between Parallel Conductors |
| **Electrodynamics** | Faraday's Law · Motional EMF & Lenz's Law · RC/RL Transients · LCR Series Resonance |
| **EM Waves & Propagation** | Maxwell's Equations & Plane Waves · Propagation in Dielectric/Conducting Media (Skin Depth) · Reflection, Refraction & Brewster's Angle |
| **Electric & Magnetic Materials** | Dielectric Polarization (Clausius–Mossotti) · Magnetic Hysteresis & the B–H Curve |

## How to Use This Guide

Each experiment page follows the same structure: Aim → Theoretical Background → Governing Formula → Interactive
Controls → Procedure → Observation Table → Graph → Calculations → Result → Precautions → Maximum Permissible
Error → Viva-Voce Questions. Work through the simulator panel alongside the page, fill in your own observation
tables and graphs (the graph grids accept a pasted photo of any physical meter/CRO trace for tracing, exactly as
in the [Advanced Laser & Optics Learning Lab](https://skjaindr.github.io/DrSKJain-course-contents-2026-27/docs/)).

See also the [Formula Reference](formula-reference.md), [Glossary](glossary.md), and [Safety](safety.md) pages.
