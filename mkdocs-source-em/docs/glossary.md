# Glossary of Key Terms

Definitions of the recurring physical concepts used across the 18 experiments and 7 workshops in this guide, in
alphabetical order. Each entry links back to the experiment(s) where the term is most central.

## A

**Ampere's circuital law** — $\oint \vec B\cdot d\vec l = \mu_0 I_{enc}$; used to find the field of highly symmetric current distributions. See [Solenoid & Toroid](experiments/ampere-law-solenoid-toroid.md).

**Atomic/molecular polarizability (α)** — The induced dipole moment per unit local electric field experienced by a single atom or molecule. See [Dielectric Polarization](experiments/dielectric-polarization-clausius-mossotti.md).

## B

**Biot–Savart law** — Gives the magnetic field contribution from an infinitesimal current element; the magnetostatic analogue of Coulomb's law. See [Biot–Savart](experiments/biot-savart-wire.md).

**Bound charge** — Charge that appears at the surface (or within the volume) of a polarized dielectric due to the alignment of its internal dipoles, as distinct from free charge. See [Dielectric Polarization](experiments/dielectric-polarization-clausius-mossotti.md).

**Brewster's angle (θ_B)** — The angle of incidence at which p-polarized light is not reflected at all at a dielectric interface. See [Reflection, Refraction & Brewster's Angle](experiments/reflection-refraction-brewster.md).

## C

**Clausius–Mossotti relation** — Connects the macroscopic dielectric constant to the microscopic atomic polarizability, including the local-field correction. See [Dielectric Polarization](experiments/dielectric-polarization-clausius-mossotti.md).

**Coercivity (H_c)** — The reverse magnetizing field required to bring the flux density of a magnetized material back to zero. See [Magnetic Hysteresis](experiments/magnetic-hysteresis-bh-curve.md).

**Coulomb's law** — The force between two point charges is proportional to the product of the charges and inversely proportional to the square of their separation. See [Coulomb's Law](experiments/coulombs-law.md).

## D

**Dielectric constant (ε_r)** — The relative permittivity of a material; the factor by which it increases a capacitor's capacitance relative to vacuum. See [Parallel-Plate Capacitor](experiments/parallel-plate-capacitor.md).

**Dipole moment (p)** — A vector p = q·(2a) characterising the strength and orientation of an electric dipole. See [Electric Dipole](experiments/dipole-field-potential.md).

## E

**Eddy current** — A circulating induced current set up in a bulk conductor by a changing magnetic flux, used constructively in eddy-current testing and braking, and minimised in transformer cores. See [WS4: Eddy-Current NDT](workshops/eddy-current-ndt.md).

## F

**Faraday's law** — The EMF induced in a circuit equals the negative rate of change of magnetic flux linkage through it. See [Faraday's Law](experiments/faraday-induction.md).

## G

**Gauss's law** — The net electric flux through any closed surface equals the enclosed charge divided by ε₀. See [Gauss's Law](experiments/gauss-law-flux.md).

## H

**Helmholtz coils** — A pair of identical coaxial coils separated by a distance equal to their radius, producing a highly uniform field at their common centre. See [Circular Coil & Helmholtz Coils](experiments/circular-coil-helmholtz.md).

**Hysteresis loop** — The closed B–H curve traced by a ferromagnetic material as the applied field is cycled; its enclosed area equals the energy dissipated per cycle. See [Magnetic Hysteresis](experiments/magnetic-hysteresis-bh-curve.md).

## L

**Lenz's law** — The induced current always flows in the direction that opposes the change in flux producing it. See [Faraday's Law](experiments/faraday-induction.md) and [Motional EMF](experiments/motional-emf-lenz.md).

## M

**Maxwell's equations** — The four coupled equations (Gauss's law for E, Gauss's law for B, Faraday's law, Ampere–Maxwell law) that fully describe classical electromagnetism and predict electromagnetic waves. See [Maxwell's Equations & Plane Waves](experiments/maxwell-plane-waves.md).

**Motional EMF** — The EMF generated in a conductor moving through a magnetic field, ε = BLv for a rod on rails. See [Motional EMF](experiments/motional-emf-lenz.md).

## Q

**Quality factor (Q)** — A dimensionless measure of the sharpness of an LCR circuit's resonance, Q = ω₀L/R. See [LCR Resonance](experiments/lcr-resonance.md).

## R

**Retentivity / Remanence (B_r)** — The flux density remaining in a ferromagnetic material after the applied field is removed. See [Magnetic Hysteresis](experiments/magnetic-hysteresis-bh-curve.md).

## S

**Skin depth (δ)** — The depth in a conductor at which an EM wave's amplitude falls to 1/e of its surface value. See [Skin Depth](experiments/wave-propagation-media-skindepth.md).

## T

**Time constant (τ)** — The characteristic time scale of an RC or RL transient (τ = RC or τ = L/R) at which the exponential response reaches (1 − 1/e) of its final change. See [RC/RL Transients](experiments/rc-rl-transients.md).

## V

**VSWR (Voltage Standing-Wave Ratio)** — A measure of impedance mismatch on a transmission line; VSWR = 1 for a perfectly matched load. See [WS7: RF Impedance Matching](workshops/rf-impedance-matching-vswr.md).
