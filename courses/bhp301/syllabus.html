BHP301Electricity & Magnetism

B.Sc. (Hons.) Physics — Semester III · L-4 T-2 P-0, Credits: 6 (Theory + Practical component)

Reproduced from the official Scheme of Instruction & Syllabi published by Invertis University (invertisuniversity.ac.in) for this course.

Unit 1 — Electrostatics (13 contact hrs)

  • Electric field, electric field lines, electric flux, Gauss's law with applications to spherical, cylindrical and planar charge distributions
  • Conservative nature of the electrostatic field, electrostatic potential
  • Laplace's and Poisson's equations, the Uniqueness theorem
  • Potential and electric field of a dipole; force and torque on a dipole in an external field
  • Capacitance of a system of charged conductors; capacitors and energy stored in a capacitor

Unit 2 — Dielectric Properties of Matter (10 contact hrs)

  • Electric field in matter: polarization P, electric displacement D
  • Relation between D, E and P; Gauss's law in the presence of dielectrics
  • Boundary conditions on D and E at a dielectric interface
  • Parallel plate capacitor completely and partially filled with dielectric
  • Energy in the presence of dielectrics

Unit 3 — Magnetostatics (12 contact hrs)

  • Magnetic field B, Lorentz force law, force on a current-carrying conductor in a magnetic field
  • Biot–Savart law and its simple applications, Ampere's circuital law and its applications
  • Vector potential; force and torque on a magnetic dipole
  • Magnetic properties of matter: magnetization M, magnetic intensity H
  • Magnetic susceptibility and permeability; classification of materials: dia-, para- and ferro-magnetic

Unit 4 — Electromagnetic Induction & AC Circuits (13 contact hrs)

  • Faraday's law of electromagnetic induction, Lenz's law
  • Self and mutual inductance; energy stored in a magnetic field
  • Alternating currents: representation of sinusoidal waveforms, peak and RMS values, phasor representation
  • AC circuits: reactance and impedance of R, L, C, RL, RC and RLC series/parallel circuits, resonance
  • Power in AC circuits: real, reactive and apparent power, power factor

Unit 5 — Network Theorems & Introduction to Maxwell's Equations (12 contact hrs)

  • Network theorems: Kirchhoff's laws, Thevenin's theorem, Norton's theorem
  • Superposition theorem, Maximum Power Transfer theorem
  • Displacement current, equation of continuity
  • Maxwell's equations in integral and differential form
  • Introduction to electromagnetic waves in vacuum; Poynting vector
